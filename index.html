About Me

Born and raised in sunny San Diego, my life in the Big Apple started when I went to graduate school at Teachers College to study music education. Back then the neigborhood was radically different than it is today. "Staying Alive" was more than just a disco song. My early years on W 122nd Street were a shocking change from watching sunsets over the Pacific. Over the years things have quieted down, though I miss all the great jazz at the West End Cafe and the family stores along Broadway.

Living on the Upper West Side has been a great experience and I never seem to tire of the pace and seeing things change. Same goes for my career, which has shape-shifted from teaching through various roles in advertising technologies. Along the way I raised a daughter who has taken up teaching as her (first) career. She got me started coaching soccer and making yoga a regular practice.

One thread through all the years has been my fascination with computers and the magic of coding. To me, coding is like composing music, constructing something as elegant as it is useful or meaningful. I look forward to doing it full time.
